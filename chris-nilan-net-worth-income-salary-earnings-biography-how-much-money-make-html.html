Christopher John Nilan (born February 9, 1958) is a former professional ice hockey player who is now a radio presenter. He played for the Chicago Blackhawks and the New York Rangers. The Montreal Canadiens, the Boston Bruins, and the New York Rangers were among the teams that Nilan represented in the National Hockey League (NHL) throughout his career, which spanned from 1980 to 1992. He was a member of the Montreal Canadiens when they won the Stanley Cup in 1986. He was affectionately known as “Knuckles” and was well-known for his proclivity for fighting. This biography of Nilan is featured extensively in Alex Gibney’s documentary film ‘The Last Gladiators’, which premiered at the 2011 Sundance Film Festival.
