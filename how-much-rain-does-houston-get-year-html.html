Houston: Hold My Beer... And Pool Noodle... When it Comes to Rain

Houston, the city with a heart of gold and a drainage system that's constantly being tested. We all know it rains a lot in Houston, but just how much is a whole other story. Buckle up, because we're about to dive into the hilarious world of Houston's annual rainfall.

So, How Much Rain Does Houston Get? The Short Answer (Kind Of)

The average annual rainfall in Houston is a whopping 49.77 inches. That's almost 4 feet of rain every single year! Just imagine, that's like taking a kiddie pool, filling it to the brim, and then dumping it on your head... every. single. year. Maybe that's why Houston's hairstylists are so good at dealing with frizz.

But Here's the Thing: It Rains Like Crazy Sometimes

Houston rainfall isn't known for its meekness. It doesn't just sprinkle, it pours. We're talking sheets of rain, buckets of rain, and sometimes even rain that feels like it's coming sideways. Remember that time it rained so hard it flooded the freeway? Yeah, that was just a Tuesday in Houston.

The Upside of All This Rain (Yes, There Really is One)

Now, before you start packing your swimsuit and moving to Atlantis, there is a silver lining to all this rain. For starters, Houston is pretty darn green. All that rain means lush parks, gorgeous trees, and plenty of places to escape the concrete jungle (when it's not, you know, flooded). Plus, hey, at least your car gets a free wash every few weeks, right?

So, How Should You Prepare for Houston's Rainy Season?

Here are a few tips for surviving (and maybe even thriving) in Houston's rainy season:

  • Invest in a good raincoat and umbrella. And maybe a pool noodle for good measure.
  • Learn to love gumboots. They may not be the height of fashion, but they'll keep your feet dry when the streets turn into rivers.
  • Download a flood warning app. This way, you'll know when to avoid the areas that turn into lakes every time there's a downpour.
  • Embrace the fact that Houston weather is unpredictable. Sunshine one minute, torrential downpour the next? That's just how we roll in H-Town.

Houston's rain may be legendary, but it's also one of the things that makes our city unique. So, next time you're caught in a downpour, just remember: it's all part of the Houston charm (and by charm, we mean slightly-waterlogged sense of humor).
