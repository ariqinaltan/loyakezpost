Trisha Stratford Death: How MAFS Relationship Expert Dr Trisha Stratford Died?

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Trisha Stratford, a renowned New Zealand-born clinical neuropsychologist, and television personality, recently passed away at the age of 72. Her demise has sent ripples across the Australian entertainment industry, and the void she leaves behind is immeasurable.

A Pillar of Married At First Sight Australia

Trisha Stratford gained significant recognition as an expert on Channel 9’s popular dating program, ‘Married At First Sight Australia’ (MAFS). Since its inception in 2015, she remained an integral part of the show until her exit in 2020. Alongside fellow experts, her input and insights added depth and credibility to the unique reality show format.

Her colleague from MAFS, John Aiken, took to social media to express his grief over her passing. Their shared experiences over seven seasons were evident in his heartfelt tribute, where he reminisced about Stratford’s love for New Zealand, relationships, and her passions, such as the All Blacks rugby team and traveling.

Channel Nine’s Tribute to a Television Icon

The Channel 9 network, home to MAFS, expressed their sorrow in a formal statement. The network extended their condolences to Stratford’s family, emphasizing the significant loss to the broader television community.

Beyond Television: Stratford’s Noteworthy Contributions

Apart from her television contributions, Stratford was an active advocate for social justice. In 2020, she decided to leave MAFS, expressing her intent to focus more on writing, research, and neuropsychotherapy. While the departure seemed amicable, Stratford later indicated reservations about the program’s direction, highlighting her commitment to maintaining both professional and personal integrity.

It was during this period that she shifted back to New Zealand, joining her Auckland-based boyfriend, Roger. Despite her prominence in television, Stratford’s contributions to the field of neuropsychology and the neuroscience of relationships cannot be understated. She authored multiple books, diving deep into the intricacies of human relationships from a scientific perspective.

A Life of Diverse Pursuits

Before her impactful tenure on MAFS, Stratford showcased her versatility by working as a war correspondent for ’60 Minutes’ in conflict regions like Somalia and Bosnia. These experiences, coupled with her academic pursuits, provided her with a unique perspective on human behavior and relationships.

Moreover, Stratford’s personal life was enriched with family bonds. She left behind a daughter, Gina, and was a doting grandmother.

In Remembrance

The passing of Trisha Stratford marks the end of an era for many fans of MAFS and those who revered her work in neuropsychology. While she will be remembered for her television appearances, her legacy is multifaceted. From war zones to the intricate neural pathways of love, Stratford’s journey and contributions remain etched in the annals of both entertainment and science. Her life serves as an inspiration, a testament to the myriad ways one can touch lives and make a lasting impact.
